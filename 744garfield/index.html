The main floor centers on a tall living room with a vaulted ceiling and a wrought-iron loft railing wrapping the second story. Two upholstered sofas convert to sleepers; a jute rug and a round wood coffee table anchor the room without crowding it.

The kitchen was remodeled in September 2023 — cream raised-panel cabinets, granite-look countertops, stainless appliances, and a breakfast bar with seating for two. A wide window over the sink looks out onto a small garden patch; the room opens directly to the dining area.

Three bedrooms, each with a queen bed. One on the main floor (good for guests who'd rather skip the stairs), two upstairs sharing a renovated bath with chrome fixtures and a quartz-style vanity. The upstairs primary has a writing desk and its own wall-mounted TV.

The garage matters here. Street parking on this stretch of Garfield gets tight — having two covered spaces plus a Level 2 EV charger is a practical luxury, especially for guests arriving from LAX or down from the Bay.

Outside, a small backyard. Not the headline feature — but a quiet spot for morning coffee, and far enough from the street that you forget you're four blocks from one of the SGV's most-loved restaurant rows.
