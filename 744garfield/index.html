The main floor centers on a tall living room with a vaulted ceiling and a wrought-iron loft railing wrapping the second story. Brand-new flooring runs through the entire home — installed during the 2023 remodel, light tone, easy to clean, soft underfoot. A guest bedroom sits off the main floor for parents, visitors, or anyone who'd rather skip the stairs.

The kitchen — cream raised-panel cabinets, granite-look countertops, stainless appliances, terrazzo-pattern tile, and a breakfast bar with seating for two. A wide window over the sink looks out onto a small garden patch; the room opens directly to the dining area. A full whole-kitchen water filtration system is already installed — drinking, cooking, and rice-washing without a Brita pitcher in sight.

Three private bedrooms. One on the main floor, two upstairs sharing a renovated bath with chrome fixtures and a quartz-style vanity. The upstairs primary has a writing desk and a wall-mounted TV — easy to convert to a child's room, a home office, or a guest suite for visiting family.

The garage is the quiet superpower of this house. Street parking on this stretch of Garfield gets tight — two attached covered spaces plus a Level 2 EV charger is the kind of detail you stop appreciating until you don't have it. Mature trees on the back wall add real privacy.

Outside, a small backyard — a quiet spot for morning coffee, four blocks from Garfield Park and walking distance to some of the best dim sum, hand-pulled noodles, and bubble tea in California.

And above all of it, a roof that was largely replaced in 2025. The single most expensive maintenance item on a townhouse — and the one most likely to surprise a first-time buyer — is already off the table for the next decade-plus.
