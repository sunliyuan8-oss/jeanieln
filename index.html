4.59 unincorporated acres in the San Martin area — county jurisdiction, no city. Two separately-addressed Craftsman residences (2005 and 2007 Jeanie Lane), both fully permitted by Santa Clara County, plus a working Zinfandel vineyard and a private water well on-site.

Imagine pushing open hand-crafted antique doors and stepping into a two-story library that pulls morning light through clerestory windows onto warm hardwood floors — a 2010 home with the soul of a much older one.

2005 Jeanie Lane, the main house, lives like a modern home with the warmth of an older one. A sunken living room with a stone fireplace. Hand-carved built-ins throughout. A bright, open kitchen with a butler's pantry — the kind of room where guests end up with a glass in hand long before dinner.

Six bedrooms, every one en-suite. The primary is a wing of its own: tray ceilings, French doors to the garden, soaking tub, dual showers, walk-in closet, marble floors with radiant heat. Three more bedrooms upstairs — two with private balconies — plus a game room with a wet bar that opens onto a deck above the vineyard.

2007 Jeanie Lane is your own historic 1,500-sqft cottage — its own address, its own porch, two bedrooms, and an updated kitchen. Together the two homes give you eight bedrooms and ten bathrooms — room for three or four generations, or a long weekend with every friend you have.

Jeanie Lane is a private road. No commuter traffic, no through-route. Mature oaks and the vineyard screen the property on every side. You'll hear the vines move before you hear a car.

4.59 flat, fully usable acres, unincorporated Santa Clara County — no city overlay, Rural Residential zoning, private well, SB9-eligible. A planted Zinfandel vineyard. An oak grove for ceremonies. A stone patio that seats twenty under string lights. Thirty minutes to downtown San Jose. Most days, you won't need it.

Three ways
to own this.

The dual-unit structure — and the event-venue layer — make this property unusually flexible. Pick the thesis that matches your strategy.

Path A · Investor

Inherit the operation.

Adopt the existing system: same cleaners, same pricing engine, same calendar. With 2007 now contributing and weekend events layered in, projected combined gross approaches $220K+ annualized. Operator notes, vendor list, and SOPs included.

Path B · Multi-generational

Three generations,
one private road.

Eight bedrooms, ten baths, two front doors, one driveway. Grandparents in 2007 with their own entrance. Parents in the main house. Kids and cousins upstairs. The kind of compound that finally makes three- or four-generation living feel easy — without anyone feeling crowded.

Path C · Wedding venue

Lean into events.

Multi-day weekend buyouts at venue rates ($8K–$15K) instead of nightly STR. The infrastructure is in place — driveway, RV pad, lawn, vineyard photo backdrop, two homes for the wedding party. A natural pivot for the right operator.
